NOTE: I will only bother putting games I absolutely loved at a 9/10 and above rating. Games surrounded with the 👑 emoji is my all-time favorite, and games marked with the color green are what I'm currently playing.

Personal Favorites (10/10)

👑 Hollow Knight 👑 - If there's something that I'd have to call truly flawless, it has to be Hollow Knight. The amount of balance between its challenges as well as playability makes for an insanely fun experience, simply put, it's perfect. Every bossfight is extremely fun and challenging, without being enough to make me stop playing it nor does it overly frustrate me.

Even if you find the gameplay concepts to be a little frustrating, there's always something to love about this game. Another one in particular, is how well this game handles its somber tone and atmosphere. The soundtrack's melody mainly consists of quiet, sad tunes that helps elevate the gloomy setting of Hollow Knight, and the underlying lore is one that will make you feel melancholic and want for more.

It's amazing, the fact that this game is made by a team of THREE people, yet it's significantly better than the majority of AAA games out there that are made by multimillion dollar companies. This is a great example of what a game can be if it's made out of pure passion, without cash in mind. This game deserves more than the price it asks for, really.

Terraria - Used to be my favorite game of all-time, and it lasted for a long while too. Endlessly fun with a large variety of things to experiment with. Amazing exploration content, unique combat, awesome bossfights, great underlying lore, this game has everything I could possibly ask for. Sadly it has to leave the throne due to my love for Hollow Knight, but this game will continue to be one of my favorites.

So far I've only played a few hours, but it's definitely fucking fantastic so far.

Other games I really loved and enjoyed (9/10)

Plants vs. Zombies - The nostalgia factor may have made my opinion of this game a little biased, but there's no denying this game's greatness.

This was instantly my favorite game back then when I was a child. Even if some of its gameplay concepts were a little intimidating, this game is really accessible for everyone and there's just the right amount of challenge for people who are new to videogames as well.

And on top of that, the soundtrack fucking slaps. It never becomes too unbearable to hear and has mostly felt chill and relaxing to listen to, even the most tension-filled songs in the album are quite calming to listen to.

Overall, if you want a break from stressful FPS games like VALORANT or something similar, PvZ is a great choice because it's a very relaxing game to play.

VALORANT - It's a strange feeling coming from a CS:GO fan who thought would hate this game due to how imbalanced it looks like.

I really love how much diversity the game has when it comes to its agents. When comparing this to CS:GO, this game easily beats it due to the amount of new content there is. I mean think about it, the Counter-Strike franchise itself has had the same gameplay for 20 years now, which has me really surprised considering how long that game maintained its popularity.

Counter-Strike: Global Offensive - The one game that manages to maintain its popularity despite seemingly not changing for more than 10 years. The gameplay is so simple, yet it has such a high skill ceiling that its still incredibly entertaining as an esport, with a lot of pros continuing to make new and unique plays.

However, with Valve pretty much not making an effort to even keep this game alive, there's pretty much 0 new content in the past few years despite continuing to release new community-made maps. The whole repeating format of "new map -> learn smokes and angles -> map mastered" is becoming stale and doesn't really provide anything to spice up the game anymore.

Portal 1 - An all-time classic. It's never too late to play something as old as this, because until now I still find it to be one of the most fun games ever.

Its puzzles are like no other. There's always a certain challenge in one way or another without being too hard or complicated even for dumb people. Games with puzzles can be really hard to get into, because you'll either get bored or frustrated whenever you're stuck at a puzzle that literally feels like a wall, but I never felt that in Portal 1.

There's a great balance between its difficulty and accessibility, which is why this game still ended up being part of mainstream despite how hard it can be to get into puzzle games. It doesn't feel too punishing, and the end result is always so satisfying.

If I ever had to criticize this game, it'll probably be the fact that it's too short. I am always desperate for more content whenever I fall in love with any game, but unfortunately, Portal 1 falls short when it comes to that aspect. Nonetheless, even if it's just 2 hours of gameplay from start to finish, you will always get maximum enjoyment from this game.

ULTRAKILL - This game probably has the BEST singleplayer combat I've ever seen. The fast-paced combat and difficulty of this game is one of my favorite aspects that really keeps me consistently hyped for the next level. The bossfights feel genuinely unique and really immerses you so well in the game because of how fast shit happens.

Portal 2 - I loved Portal 1, and this game definitely lived up to my expectations as well, but I can't really say it's better than its prequel.

In its core, it's still Portal in a lot of ways despite some of its major changes, it's still the same old Portal with cool puzzles + new concepts.

The game is also longer now, and it certainly works this way because of the added narrative, cool cinematography, and new puzzle concepts that are pretty fun to play around with. However, I felt like the devs were trying too hard to come up with tons of variety with these new concepts, and it's why some levels end up being very frustrating to play with. The one thing the original Portal was the strongest at ended up disappearing from the 2nd game, which is the balance between difficulty and accessibility.

I also really missed the old atmosphere in the old Portal. Now I know Valve is trying to make the game more accessible by getting rid of the creepy atmosphere, but I always feel like the eeriness of the first game really gives out that sense of loneliness really well which I really like.

Now on to the story itself, the narrative in this game is honestly really good and is the main reason why the 2nd game is praised so much by its fans. It's not mind blowing like many people make it out to be, I really loved the development between Chell and GLaDOS' and how well this builds up to the climax, even if the rest of the story felt more like popcorn flick rather than well-thought-out writing.

In some ways, there are definitely aspects of the game that are really improved upon the first game. On the other hand, I still can't help but feel like something is missing. Nonetheless, it's still very much Portal through its core in every way possible, and if you're a fan of the first game, you'll definitely still love this one.
