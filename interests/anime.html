NOTE: I will only bother putting anime I absolutely loved at a 9/10 and above rating. Anime surrounded with the 👑 emoji is my all-time favorite, and anime marked with the color green are what I'm currently watching.

Personal Favorites (10/10)

👑Bocchi the Rock👑 - Wholeheartedly my favorite anime forever. A chaotic yet extremely comforting comedy coupled with heartwarming characters. AND THE FUCKING MEMES!!! Despite being mostly comedy, I can't help but relate to Bocchi. Really helped get my shit together for once. I'll forever be thankful for this show.

Steins;Gate - Incredibly complex, yet surprisingly accessible and easy to digest. One of the few shows that get time travel right without being so convoluted, alongside a great story and some of the best character dynamic I've seen from Okabe and Kurisu (my favorite pair). Steins;Gate is the one anime that stood really well against the test of time, it continued to be my favorite anime for a year of me watching anime before I discovered Bocchi.

Girls' Last Tour - A melancholic and gloomy world that manages to be so wholesome and comforting despite its premise. Really different compared to the usual CGDCT show out there. It shows a story of two girls trying to find hope in a tragic world.

The Disappearance of Haruhi Suzumiya - This movie takes all the good aspects of the Haruhi franchise and greatly improves upon it even more. The characters are now believable, the story has now leaned towards a more dramatic tone, and the mysteries and plot twists end up being more compelling than it already was. Kyon has the best character development I've ever seen.

Oregairu Season 2 - A romance drama that's easy to brush off as simply another edgy high school anime with a narcissistic protagonist. It's far from it, though. It is ultimately one of the anime that realistically portrays "teenager drama" really well, as well as the underlying motives of society, without being extremely edgy in its premise. Hachiman is one of the few protagonists I strongly sympathize with, and his character development is something to learn from as he moves on from his narcissistic self and opens up to his real friends about his struggles for once.

Hunter x Hunter (2011) - I don't usually like long-running action shounen anime, considering how most of them seem to drag on way too much for me. HxH is different due to its extremely compelling characters and a well-structured power system that works well to flesh out each character. Its fights aren't anything "flashy" like Demon Slayer, but it's thrilling because of the mind games of its compelling power system.

Other anime I really loved and enjoyed (9/10)

Gintama - Besides Bocchi the Rock!, this is probably one of the funniest comedy I've seen. The fact that this is a long running anime makes it a much more worthwhile experience, because in like 300+ episodes, there's no way you won't find a single episode that's your cup of tea. Gintama loves to parody various different shows as well as some subtle references which makes some of its humor even more hilarious. My only gripes with Gintama is its inconsistency with the humor, and some of the serious arcs can get overly cringe.

Mushishi - I haven't finished it yet, but it's very calming to watch without being too boring and uneventful. A great balance between tension and a calming atmosphere, which is the reason why I find this anime so accessible despite my dislike for iyashikei. Ginko is such a cool dude.

The Garden of Sinners (Chapter 5) - First time I actually enjoyed a confusing anime. While the characters aren't particularly well-developed apart from Enjou, the achronological ordering of the scenes just made the mysteries and plot twists 10x more impactful than it already is.

Bloom Into You - Even as someone mostly indifferent to this show's genre, there's no denying this show's greatness. Past its simple and romantic premise shows profound themes of understanding one's self, whether it be your sexuality, love, or just finding one's purpose in life.

Puella Magi Madoka Magica - A dark anime that actually manages to not be obnoxiously edgy! It's a fantastic contrast to the usual lightheared magical girls show. Rather than a show full of hope, Madoka Magica presents theme of hopelessness and despair instead, without being unnecessarily edgy for the sake of it. Its darkest moments have meaning and serves a purpose rather than being the typical torture porn for the sake of shock value.

Gurren Lagann - Funny enough, the anime I covered just before this (Madoka Magica) is an anime full of hopelessness. Then we got this anime, an anime full of hope (and chadness too), like literally. It's such a fucking absurd show, but it works so well with what its trying to portray. "Don’t believe in yourself. Believe in me! Believe in the Kamina who believes in you!"

March comes in like a lion - Quite possibly the anime I have the most respect for, even if it's not my all-time favorite. I have so much respect for this because of how much it portrays depression so well, without being very dark or hard to watch. In fact, this anime is actually pretty lighthearted, yet its THE anime that depicts depression the best. Not a personal fan of its slow pacing, but it works best for the show's narrative approach so it's just a personal thing (more like I have bad attention span haha).

Monogatari Series - Most bizarre shit I've watched, for a good reason. It's quite possibly the most unique anime I've seen with how it plays around with the visuals as a unique way of storytelling, and its dialogue is some of the best I've seen. I'm not usually a fan of extreme walls of dialogue when it comes to anime, but Monogatari managed to make it so compelling to watch. Its excessive fanservice and loli fetish are my only gripes with this otherwise masterpiece-level story.

Kaguya-sama: Love is War - If Gintama is too inconsistent for you, Kaguya-sama on the other hand is a consistently engaging psychological comedy that really keeps you hyped up for the silly mind games of the main couple. Its humor is hilarious and sometimes references some popular shows just like Gintama does. Oh, and it has some cool serious moments too. There's very few of them in Kaguya-sama though as compared to Gintama.

Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood - Setting aside the first few, terribly paced episodes, this is basically a much better AOT (although they're not really the same anime). Both anime can be very political with some of the best mysteries and plot twists in anime I've seen. I personally prefer FMAB, because there's more emphasis on proper character growth, and the overall experience was a lot more consistent for me especially since it actually has a good ending unlike AOT.

Clannad: After Story - I still stand by the fact that the first anime was extremely boring and repetitive, but After Story was a different. It's an extremely beautiful story that really does well at portraying the struggles of adulthood. It's just a shame that you have to go through 24 episodes worth of dull moments just to reach After Story, but it's still a worthwhile experience regardless.

A Silent Voice - Will also make you shed tears like a baby just like Clannad, except A Silent Voice as a whole is much more coherent with what needs to be conveyed and avoids meandering around for too long. The visuals and OST of the anime makes the overall product even more impactful than it already is, which is also the main reason why I prefer the anime over the manga despite what the fandom thinks otherwise. The characters are a little underdeveloped considering the runtime, but the two protagonists is enough to keep you connected to the story.

Yuru Camp - Looking for an anime to calm yourself down and take a break from most tension-filled shows? Then you're right at home. Yuru Camp fully embraces its slice-of-life format to the max, and even though there's "nothing" really happening, you can't help but feel so calm just watching the girls camp and eat food. Its sweet and serene soundtrack accompanies this really well and gives off an overall relaxing atmosphere.

K-On! - Also very similar to Yuru Camp, and a bit of Bocchi the Rock too. Except this time, the setting is in high school rather than camping. Unfortunately if you're a big fan of music anime, there's a bigger ratio of girls drinking tea here instead of actually playing music compared to Bocchi the Rock. It essentially accomplishes the same thing as Yuru Camp, except it's more so focused on the characters instead of a serene atmosphere like Yuru Camp, therefore there is a bigger emphasis on slow character build-up to make you appreciate the characters more.

Attack on Titan (Pre-season 4) - If you're a sucker for good mysteries and plot twists, then this is a great one. It's extremely consistent with its surprises and emotional beats, and the end result always turns out to be incredibly rewarding for the plot. The character writing certainly needs some more work, but otherwise the plot carries the show well enough.

Vanitas no Carte - An anime about badass and horny vampires. Jokes aside, as a mystery anime, this one's also just as good as Attack on Titan. Even better, is that the characters feel a lot more real here and is developed really well compared to AOT. The comedy is really bad though and it can get really distracting in the midst of a serious moment.

Fate/Zero - Basically Battle Royale in anime. There's a great variety of memorable characters, and every servant is always really well fleshed out. The fights are amazing even for its age, too. This is quite possibly the only entry in the fate franchise I liked, and the fact that it's written by a different author is honestly impressive.

Hibike! Euphonium - I've seen plenty of anime with fantastic visuals and great attention to detail, but no anime has ever come close to KyoAni's passion for Hibike Euphonium, not even Bocchi the Rock which is my favorite music anime. Of course, there's no denying that the OST is fucking amazing too, as expected from a music anime. Besides the greatness of the adaptation, the story is amazing and I love Kumiko and Reina's relationship.

A Place Further Than The Universe - When I first watched it, I never really regarded it to be as special as everyone makes it out to be, even until now. Themes of friendship is generally always recycled in anime like this, but I understood more about this show after a rewatch. I really admire how genuine and earnest this anime is, and most of all it's extremely comforting. Sorayori most of all, is about an adventure; an adventure through self-discovery.
